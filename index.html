Whether it's getting up early to host a hump day or post-hump day mornings show on FBi, siphoning energy into her Sittin' Pretty events, or remaining cool calm and collected with only half a pair of USBs, Eddy Diamond is the musical force we're eternally grateful to have in our lives.

Her work both inside and out of the music scene, be it throwing gigs that showcase upcoming talent or helping organise community projects, help ensure the many voices of different groups in Sydney are heard and are a testament to the impact that a person can have when truly engaged in their community.

Not afraid to jump genres when it feels right, and equipped with a prescient knowledge of many scenes under the moon, we couldn't think of anyone better to join the dots for us between this decade and the next.

After a recent trip to suss out what our crates have to offer, Martha has decided to return north once more this year to inspect the state of the dancers as well.

Currently residing in Melbourne, she has swiftly earned a reputation for her specialist twelve inch selections, crafty overlaying of spoken samples, and seamless transitions between BRUK, breaks, drum n bass and jazz numbers.

Combining this rap with with her truly underground mixes as Subterranean Jazz Club, expect a Sydney debut set that melds a wholesome musical summary of the year just passed with a crystal clear vision of the future sounds ahead.
