Whether it's getting up early to host a hump day or post-hump day mornings show on FBi, siphoning energy into her Sittin' Pretty events, or remaining cool calm and collected with only half a pair of USBs, Eddy Diamond is the musical force we're eternally grateful to have in our lives.

Her work both inside and out of the music scene, whether it be throwing gigs that showcase upcoming talent or helping organise community projects, help ensure the many voices of different groups in Sydney are heard and are a testament to the impact that a person can have when truly engaged in their community.

Not afraid to jump genres when it feels right, and equipped with a prescient knowledge of many scenes under the moon, we couldn't think of anyone better to join the dots for us between this decade and the next.

After a recent trip to Sydney suss out what our crates have to offer, Martha has decided to return once more this year to inspect the state of the dancers as well.

Over the last 12 months, she has swiftly earned a reputation for specialist twelve inch selections, and seamless transitions through an array of BRUK, breaks, drum n bass and jazz records.

Combining this rap with with her truly underground mixes as Subterranean Jazz Club, we can't wait for her to meld a wholesome musical summary of the year behind us with a crystal clear vision of the hot notes we're to hear in the year ahead.
